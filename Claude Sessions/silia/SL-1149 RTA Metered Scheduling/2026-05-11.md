### Step 1: Diagnosed the problem
- **What:** User showed Stripe dashboard screenshot — an RTA subscription schedule (sub_sched_) for May 15 had only a $0.00 MXN/month flat price, no metered price

- **Why:** Mirror the same behavior as `createSubscription()` for immediate subscriptions
- **Files touched:** Billing/infrastructure/gateways/StripeGateway.ts lines 339-410
- **Result:** Schedule phases now include metered price items

### Step 3: Passed meteredPriceId to createScheduledSubscription
- **What:** Changed `createSubscription.ts` to pass `meteredPriceId ? [meteredPriceId] : undefined` and removed the warning log
- **Why:** The metered price was being pre-created but never passed to the scheduled subscription
- **Files touched:** Billing/infrastructure/aws/handlers/createSubscription/createSubscription.ts lines 128-141
- **Result:** Future-dated RTA subscriptions now include metered pricing

### Step 4: Fixed downstream gap — save stripeSubscriptionItemId on schedule activation

- **Why:** When schedule activates, `reportUsageToStripe` needs `stripeSubscriptionItemId` to report usage. Previously this was never set for scheduled RTA subscriptions.
- **Files touched:** Billing/application/handlers/StripeWebhookHandler.ts lines 505-537
- **Result:** Full lifecycle works: create schedule → activate → usage reporting
